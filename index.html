I am a final year CS Ph.D. student at Georgia Tech, advised by Judy Hoffman. My research interests are in developing robust computer vision systems that can be deployed in the real world. To this end, I have developed algorithms for self-supervised adaptation of visual models across spatio-temporal distribution shifts and tasks, intelligent annotation via active learning, and failure anticipation via simulating and generating challenging test cases.

I earned my Master's in CS (awarded the MS Research award) in Spring '19, also at Georgia Tech, where I was advised by Devi Parikh and worked on developing visual conversational agents. In grad school, I've had the opportunity to intern at NVIDIA (with Sanja Fidler), Salesforce (with Nikhil Naik), and Curai (with Anitha Kannan). Before that, I've had stints as a research assistant at Virginia Tech (with Dhruv Batra) and Adobe, and as a mentor for Google Summer of Code. Prior to that, I received my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from BITS Pilani.

Update: I'm on the industry job market! Please reach out (virajp@gatech.edu) if you think I might be a good fit for your team.
