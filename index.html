My broad interests lie in computer vision and natural language processing, particularly in applying deep learning approaches to high level computer vision and multimodal learning tasks. I am interested in building machines that can emulate the remarkable human ability to combine sensory inputs to develop a semantic understanding of the natural world.

Since August 2016, I'm a visiting student in the Machine Learning and Perception lab at Virginia Tech, working with Prof. Dhruv Batra and Prof. Devi Parikh on problems at the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing. Prior to this, I worked as a front-end and Android developer at Adobe Systems India on Adobe Captivate Prime, a newly launched SAAS-based Learning Management System for enterprise.

I graduated with a Bachelor's in computer science from BITS Pilani in July 2015. Over the course of my undergraduate studies, I had the chance to work on several interesting projects in machine learning and computer vision. For a detailed list, please take a look at my CV.

I'm an AI enthusiast and find computer vision particularly fascinating, and enjoy building applications and working on challenging problems with real life utility. I also dabble in writing, music and quizzing.
